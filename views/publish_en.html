Cold virus

Virus · Causative organism of cold
It is released into the air by being mixed in droplets from sneeze or cough of infected man. Cold viruses enter into the body through mouth or nose of person who contacted this droplet.

Encephalomyelitis virus

Virus · Causative organism of Bird Influenza
As a virus that causes bird influenza, ataxia or paralysis may be caused in chicks. It is a second type of infectious disease in domestic animals that causes the reduction of spawning rate for matured fowl. It is very resistance to the external environment and a large amount of viruses are released through faces.

Influenza

Virus · Causative organism of flue
It is accompanied by respiratory symptom such as coughing while constitutional symptoms such as headache, fever, chill and muscular pains are suddenly appeared. In general, high fever occurs, and other symptoms may be varied depending on patients. Infection routes are mainly two which are droplets (respiratory secretion) infection and contact infection.

Dog parvo virus

Virus · Dog infectious bacteria
As an infectious virus that occurs in dogs, it is not contagious against human. It is very contagious and is spread through indirect and direct secretions. Fatality rate would be reached to 91% without vaccination.

Dog infectious virus

Virus · Causative organism of flue
Viruses are mainly caused and spread by secretions of infected animals. Recovered dogs release virus through urine for more than 6 months. Severe weakness, leukopenia and extension of bleeding time would be shown.

Colon bacillus

Bacteria · Causative organism of enteropathy
As one of bacterial classes that are found in large intestine, it is harmless to human body. However, people with reduced immunity may suffer from diseases according to the type of colon bacillus.

Staphylococcus

Bacteria · Avirulent
Most of them are soil microorganisms and are harmless to human body. It inhabits the mucous membrane or skin of human or various living things.

Staphylococcus aureus

Bacteria · Causative organism of food poisoning
It is one of staphylococcus that live in skin and digestive canal of human or animals. It causes various epidermis infections such as abscess, food poisoning, pneumonia, meningitis and septicemia to human. However, it may also exist as normal bacteria, not pathogenic bacterium.

Salmonella

Bacteria · Causative organism of food poisoning
As a representative causative organism of food poisoning, it causes typhoid and paratyphoid. It normally lives in intestines of human and animals and is naturally found in raw meat products. It enters through unsanitary food handling process.

Bacillus pyocyaneus

Bacteria · Causative organism of septicemia
As opportunistic pathogen, it is harmless to healthy people. However, it may be fatal those with reduced immunity enough to lead to death. Bacillus pyocyaneus may lead to skin diseases such as follicultitis, keratitis, pneumonia, monochamus, arthropathy and peritonitis. If patients who had surgery, burns or trauma are infected, it may be developed to septicemia and meningitis.

Mould fungus

Bacteria · Causative organism of skin diseases
Fungus has the meaning of ‘real bacteria’ compared to bacterial. As bacteria that causes great damage to human and crops, it mainly causes blast fungus of rice, dieback of various crops and Uredinales of mushrooms. To human, it causes athlete’s foot, candidiasis (mucosal inflammation), aspergillusis and Cryptococcus meningitis. Occurrence frequency is relatively low for healthy people, but it would be fatal to those with reduced immunity.

Yeast
